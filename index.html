How I Work

My Approach

I believe the most dangerous thing in data science is technical confidence without contextual understanding. A model that achieves 99% accuracy on the wrong problem is worse than no model at all.

My process always starts with one question: what decision does this need to enable? From there, I design backwards — choosing methods that are interpretable, auditable, and aligned to the people who will act on the output.

I maintain UK GDPR compliance as a design principle, not an afterthought. Data minimisation, purpose limitation, and privacy by design are embedded into everything I build — because responsible AI is not optional in 2026. It is the standard.

I am genuinely adaptable across environments and technologies. New industries, new tools, new team cultures — I approach change as an opportunity. My background across lecturing, entrepreneurship, and engineering means I have operated in radically different contexts and delivered effectively in all of them. That versatility is not a coincidence. It is a practised discipline.

01

Problem First, Technology Second

I start with the business problem — not the algorithm. The best tool is the one that solves the right problem with clarity and confidence.

02

Responsible by Design

GDPR compliance, explainability, and fairness are architectural decisions — built in from the start, never bolted on.

03

Communicate to Decide

04

Adapt and Accelerate

I learn new environments, tools, and domains rapidly and deliberately. Adaptability is a professional discipline — and one of my greatest strengths.
